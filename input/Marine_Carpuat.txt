I am an Associate Professor in Computer Science at the University of Maryland, with a joint appointment at the University of Maryland Institute for Advanced Computer Studies (UMIACS). I am a member of the CLIP lab.

My research aims to advance Human-Centered Natural Language Processing to help people communicate no matter what language they speak.

Recent Activities
12/23 We received an Outstanding Paper Award at EMNLP 2023 for "Physician Detection of Clinical Harm in Machine Translation: Quality Estimation Aids in Reliance and Backtranslation Identifies Critical Errors". Congratulations to Nikita Mehandru, Sweta Agrawal, Elaine Khoong, Niloufar Salehi for making this interdisciplinary collaboration a success!
12/23 We are presenting 8 papers at EMNLP 2023. Key themes: reliance and trust, explainability, building common ground across cultural differences, measuring harms.
